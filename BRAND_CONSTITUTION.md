# KYND: The Source of Truth (Brand Constitution v1.0)

## 1. The Core Philosophy
* **The Mission:** Making AI more human.
* **The Vision:** We don't build faster bots; we build living proxies. We capture the "human tell"—the friction, the hesitation, and the off-beat rhythm that defines us.
* **The Moat:** While the industry races toward mechanical efficiency, Kynd occupies the territory of **Human Truth**.

---

## 2. Visual Identity
### Palette: Linen & Ink
* **Linen (Background):** `#F2F0E9` — A warm, breathable off-white. Use for primary app backgrounds and parchment-style surfaces.
* **Ink (Logo & Primary Text):** `#1A1A1B` — A deep, permanent charcoal. Signals authority and craftsmanship.
* **Clay (Alerts):** `#D9775F` — Use sparingly for errors or critical calls to action. It feels like earth, not a clinical warning.

### Typography
* **Headings:** Use `Fraunces`, a high-contrast, modern Serif.
* **Body:** Use `Inter`, a clean, legible Sans Serif font.
* **Philosophy:** If it feels like a literary journal or a bespoke notebook, it is Kynd.

### The Logo: The Vessel
_Located in `public/kynd_logo.png`_
* **Shape:** A solid, monolithic, organic stone shape (the "Vessel"). 
* **Logic:** It has zero internal detail and a subtle "pulse" in its silhouette. It represents a vessel of human understanding.

---

## 3. Motion & Interaction (The Pulse)
* **Rhythm:** No "snappy" digital transitions. Use a 400ms `ease-in-out` curve that mimics a human breath.
* **States:** Use soft "cross-dissolves." Content should emerge, not slide.
* **Loading:** The Vessel logo should gently pulse (opacity 40% to 100%) in a biological rhythm.

---

## 4. Component Library
* **Primary Button:** Solid **Ink** block, **Linen** text, `12px` corner radius.
* **The Kynd Card:** Lighter Linen background, subtle border, used to house agent insights.
* **Input Fields:** A single Ink underline. The cursor is a soft, pulsing vertical line.

---

## 5. Verbal Identity
* **Tone:** Warm, grounded, observant, and sophisticated.
* **The Error Rule:** Never say "System Error." Say: *"Kynd lost the trail here. Let’s try that again."*
* **Naming:** Use descriptive, human names for personas (e.g., *The Deliberate Buyer*).
